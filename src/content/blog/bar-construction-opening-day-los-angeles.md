Bar construction is more than a good-looking dining room. Opening day depends on equipment, mechanical systems, electrical loads, plumbing, health department needs, finishes, lighting, and guest circulation working together.

Los Angeles hospitality projects also tend to involve tight schedules, multiple consultants, landlord requirements, and inspections that need coordination before the final punch list.

econstruct builds bars, restaurants, commercial kitchens, and hospitality interiors with those operating realities in mind.

## Details to coordinate

- Bar equipment and undercounter layout
- Commercial kitchen and prep requirements
- Lighting, electrical, and low-voltage systems
- Plumbing and floor sinks
- Dining room finishes and durability
- Inspection and opening schedule

## Why it matters

When the construction team understands the operational model, the space is more likely to support service, maintenance, guest experience, and the owner’s opening timeline.
